Στην Ταβέρνα

Καλώς ήρθες! Order your way through a Greek taverna — one phrase at a time, no menu-pointing required.

The sun is setting over the harbour and your stomach is growling. Down a narrow lane, lanterns glow over a little taverna where old Σωκράτης the owl is tying on his apron, ready to feed every hungry traveller who wanders in.

But the owl serves his feast in six courses, and each is locked until you can ask for it — in Greek. Not perfect grammar. Not flawless spelling. Just the warm, everyday words a traveller needs to sit down, order well, and eat like a local.

"Μη ντρέπεσαι. Don't be embarrassed —
stumbling over the words is half the fun of the meal."

Behind each course, a small puzzle. Listen to the owl speak, match the dishes to their words, choose the meaning — and earn a golden key. Collect all six, and the whole table is yours: 66 essential phrases for eating out in Greece. Πάμε; Shall we go?
